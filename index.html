Profile

Adaptation. I wasn’t satisfied playing video games, so I took a tech elective in middle school and made my own. I wasn’t satisfied doing work that felt insignificant to the bigger picture, so I worked with a team at a veterinary hospital where the stakes were so high, my input literally could mean life or death. I wasn’t satisfied with just helping my friends with their mental health, so I worked in a psychiatric hospital where it was my job to maintain empathy, compassion and understanding towards everyone's experiences. Then, after being active in the workforce for 12 years, I couldn’t be satisfied working with computers, I needed to be inside of them. I grew to not be satisfied using the code of others to design the websites I needed, so I made my own. Adaptability forces me to strive for more—because it makes me hard to please. Now that I’ve found my way into coding, my adaptability can benefit not only me, but the world around me as well.
